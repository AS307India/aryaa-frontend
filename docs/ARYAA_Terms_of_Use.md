## 1. What ARYAA Is

ARYAA is an Emergency Communication & Coordination Platform. It helps the right people receive the right information at the right time during an emergency, by coordinating alerts, location sharing, and response tools between you and the people you trust.

**ARYAA does not guarantee that help will arrive.** ARYAA's role is to maximize the chances that, if help can come, it reaches you as quickly, accurately, and intelligently as possible. It is not a replacement for calling official emergency services, and it does not itself dispatch police, medical, or fire services.

## 4. Known Limitations

ARYAA's core safety features depend on conditions outside our control. By using the app, you acknowledge and accept the following limitations, which are also disclosed to you during onboarding:

- **Network dependency.** ARYAA requires a network or data connection to send alerts, share location, and notify contacts. In areas with no signal or connectivity, alerts may be delayed until connectivity is restored.
- **Battery dependency.** A device with no battery cannot send an alert.
- **Location accuracy.** GPS accuracy varies depending on your environment (e.g., indoors, underground, dense urban areas) and is not guaranteed to be precise.
- **Lock-screen visibility.** To ensure the fastest possible response, incoming emergency alerts are visible to a Trusted Contact directly from their device's lock screen, without requiring them to unlock their device. This means anyone holding an unlocked responder's device during an active alert may also see it.
- **Tiered notification timing.** Trusted Contacts marked as "nearby" responders are notified instantly. Contacts marked as "faraway" are notified after a short delay if no nearby responder has confirmed they are responding, so that local help can be mobilized first without unnecessarily alarming distant family members.
- **No emergency-service integration.** ARYAA does not automatically contact police, ambulance, or fire services. Where the app references calling 112 or another emergency number, it opens your device's own phone dialer for you to place the call yourself — it never dials automatically without your action.
